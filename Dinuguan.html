Dinuguan

Dish Recipes:


  • 1 lb. pork shoulder diced
  • 20 oz. pork blood
  • 1/2 lb pork ears boiled and sliced
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • 1 bunch lemongrass
  • 3 long green peppers
  • 1 onion chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:


  1. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Sauté the garlic and onion.
  2. Once the onion softens, add the pork kasim. Continue sautéing for 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Add the pork ears. Sauté for 2 minutes.
  4. Pour the vinegar. Cover the pot and then let it boil. Stir.
  5. Add dried bay leaves and lemongrass. Cover the pot and adjust the heat to the lowest setting. Simmer for 50 minutes or until the pork tenderizes completely. Note: Make sure to stir occasionally. You can add water as needed.
  6. Pour the pork blood into the pot. Stir. Let the mixture boil and then simmer for 12 minutes.
  7. Add the long green pepper. Continue cooking for 3 minutes.
  8. Season with sugar, salt, and ground black pepper.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
