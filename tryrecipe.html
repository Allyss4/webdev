Holy Trinity

Artist: Masaccio

Era: Early Renaissance, c. 1427

Description (Ingredients):

  • God the Father
  • Jesus Christ
  • The Holy Spirit
  • Mary, the Mother of Christ
  • John the Evangelist
  • Two Donors (Depicted in prayer)
  • Architectural elements with classical columns and arches
  • Skeleton with inscription: "I am what you will be"

Instructions:

  1. Begin with the vertical arrangement of figures: place God the Father at the top, supporting the crucified Christ.
  2. Add the Holy Spirit, symbolized as a dove, positioned between them.
  3. Place Mary and John at the foot of the cross, gazing towards the viewer to draw them into contemplation.
  4. Incorporate the donors, kneeling in prayer, on either side of the fresco.
  5. Construct the architectural framework using linear perspective to create depth and realism.
  6. Finish with the skeleton at the bottom, adding the inscription to remind viewers of mortality and spiritual reflection.

Tip: Observe the interplay of light and shadow to enhance the presence of each figure, symbolizing divine grace and the inevitability of death.
